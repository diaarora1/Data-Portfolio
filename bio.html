About Me

I'm Dia, a student at Cerritos High School with a passion for coding, traveling, and reading.

My journey into the world of technology began during my freshman year, when I discovered the creative freedom of web development; while my first project was a simple movie site with a pink background and static posters, my work has since evolved into vibrant, interactive spaces featuring custom fonts and playful borders that make every site feel personal. Continuing to pursue my passion, I’ve found an interest in data science, coming across it during a summer course at UCLA. There, I realized that data is so much more than just numbers on a page; it is a powerful tool for social justice that can uncover hidden patterns and turn barriers into clear maps for the community.

Whether I'm analyzing DoorDash delivery times to understand correlation or connecting NYC’s public WiFi locations with Census poverty data to investigate the digital divide, I am driven by the belief that data can reveal systemic gaps and help us advocate for a more equitable future. I want to show other teenagers my age, the ones who will ultimately shape our future, that data analysis is an accessible and creative way to solve complicated problems and participate in building a more organized, predictable, and just society for everyone.
